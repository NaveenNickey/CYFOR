Forensic Report Best Practices
▸ Always include a Case ID for chain of custody
A unique Case ID ensures proper tracking of evidence throughout the investigation lifecycle. It should follow your organization's naming convention and be referenced in all documentation.
▸ Document all findings even if inconclusive
Negative results and inconclusive findings are just as important as positive results. They demonstrate thoroughness and help establish what was ruled out during the investigation.
▸ SHA-256 is the gold standard for file integrity
While MD5 and SHA-1 are included for legacy compatibility, SHA-256 is currently the recommended standard for cryptographic hashing in forensic analysis due to its collision resistance.
▸ Include investigator credentials in every report
The investigator's name and institution should be clearly documented to establish accountability and provide contact information for follow-up questions or legal proceedings.
